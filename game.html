Game Project

Marvel: War of Heroes

2015  |  Group of 15​
Unity mobile Card Game published in China and Taiwan. Based on the Disney Marvel Series.​
> Unity / Excel / MySQL / HTML / Perforce / Bugzilla
​> In-game Campaign Design / Database Mgmt / Process Mgmt
Marvel: War of Heroes was a professional game development project by XPEC Entertainment. As product manager and lead designer, I took care of both management and design work after the game's publication in mainland China and Taiwan.
> Official Site

I devised a product strategy with DeNA (the publisher) and Disney, and led level design and in-game campaign design in sync with marketing plans such as those for Avengers: Age of Ultron, which came out in 2015.

The game featured Marvel heroes and focused on how to enliven them. Our team designed character strengths, skills, and specialties according to each hero's characteristics, and recreated classic battles in the game to motivate players keep collecting and growing the characters.

However, because the enthusiasm created by Marvel movies and major Disney promotional activities often lasted no longer than a month, the game faced a highly fluctuating demand, which led very intensive activity for a short period of time, followed by long periods of inactivity. This meant that the company had to effectively accumulate as much revenue as possible during the active periods, and this made the game's response speed crucial.

To achieve this, I decided to trim the whole working process. I developed a modularized control method for the game, including standardized forms and categorized actions. It successfully shortened the time for update works by 20% and accelerated the version turnover time from two weeks to one week. This made it possible to have 4 versions of the game concurrently being maintained on iOS and Android in Mainland China and Taiwan, and achieve more than 400% ROI on advertisement investment during those periods of focused activity.

Dynasty Warriors - Zhan

2014  |  Group of 7​
Unity mobile ARPG. Based on KOEI TECMO's Dynasty Warriors series.
> Unity / Visio / Coggle / Excel / Powerpoint / JIRA
​> System Architecture / AI Design / Proposal / Strategic Planning / Financial Forecasting / Project Mgmt
Dynasty Warriors - Zhan was a professional game development project by XPEC Entertainment. The game was a mobile sequel of Dynasty Warriors, licensed by KOEI TECMO. Although I left the company in 2015, another team in XPEC is now still working on it and the expected publication will be in 2016.

The game attempted to reproduce "an atmosphere of war" in ancient China, and “the unlimited power in killing and victory”—the foremost impression of playing the Dynasty Warriors series—through classic designs, such as interactions with other generals, the growth and decline of each country’s forces, and innumerable enemy and ally units on a big battle field simultaneously.

As lead designer, I designed the system architecture with KOEI TECMO (the IP owner), and led the design especially in the core gameplay, AI, game balance, and the commercialization strategy. I analyzed key factors about why the Dynasty Warriors series succeeded over other platforms, and defined whether the team should port the mechanisms directly, reproduce them on mobile with other mobile-friendly approaches, or simply create new ones. I led a small team and built the game’s prototype from scratch. In addition, as in the early stage of development, I established a sequence of design procedures for the game systems, modularized design components, including state machine AI and level plans , and defined the primary back-end data structures.

With distinctive hardware and UI from the original version, our design team was greatly challenged when re-creating the “musou feeling”, manifested by TV’s big screen and dedicated gamepad formats. We had to draw on our experience of segregating game mechanisms from the whole, and analyzing their meaning and effects in depth.

For the iconic flow of the 0-5 ordinary attack and the special attack combo (0-5 square button then circle button on PS), lacking real button’s feedback, I proposed an analogous input, which captured the core experience that requires players to "count in the mind". While automatically performing ordinary attack combo, the player decides the timing of triggering special attacks by the "only" special attack virtual button on the screen. Additionally, depending on the number of ordinary attack combos after which the special attack button is pushed, different special attacks can be triggered, in the same way as in console versions (please refer to Game Play Recording). This attempt proved a great success, reminding the testers of the critical experience in the original games.

Tales of the Immortals

2013  |  Group of 30​
Flash 3D browser RPG published in China. Based on Chinese Daoism legends with XPEC’s Beehive Engine.​
> Excel / Powerpoint / Word / Edraw / Xmind / Photoshop / VBA / MySQL / Perforce / Bugzilla / Trello
​> System Architecture / Game Balancing and Economy Design / System Design / Info Architecture / Database Mgmt / General Mgmt / Project Mgmt / Process Mgmt / Data Mining and Analysis
Tales of the Immortals was a professional game development project by XPEC Entertainment.
​
A Flash based role playing game on XPEC’s Beehive Engine, it required no client installment, while maintaining a short loading speed, and was designed to be easily accessed anytime, anywhere.

The game applied a story based on Chinese Daoism legends, with the player as the protagonist undertaking training, gathering comrades, and passing tests to become a celestial being. Unlike common on-line games, the game did not require much interpersonal interaction. Players focused on individual character’s growth and pursued superior 3D arts and visual effects as feedback, with a carefully tendered pay-to-win economy model.

The game gained 400k accumulated members, with Retention and Payment indicators 30% above the category average. It was also remastered on Android and iOS, and exported as Vietnamese and Russian versions.

As deputy producer and lead designer, I managed the general game development work of 30+ designers, artists, and programmers. I devised the game’s product strategy with Firefox Game (the publisher), led the core game play and the game economy design with Tencent Games (the major platform/channel) in its Game Incubation Project.

What I would like to highlight about my experience gained in this project is the concurrent management of different kinds of players, distinguishable by their in-game consumption. Under the pay-to-win model, our game play specifically amplified paid players' strengths, with recognizable superiority through battles and ostentatious luxury weapons and decorations. However, we also carefully controlled the experience of the players not paying for the game by securing a basic playable path—although their progress was far slower than that of the paid players'— to keep them in the game, thus supporting a healthy and meaningful game world for the paying players.

In fact, we tried dividing our players into more than three groups according to their amount of payment, and crafted all of the game systems and economy models in accordance with the detailed controlled in-game experience for each group in the different game stages.

The approach with our economy model later became a standard foundation in the studio’s other games with similar commercialization strategies.
Game Trailer​
This shows the game's Chinese Daoism base, and its Chinese style arts in divergent game systems, which was one of the game's key characteristics. The game served as a "small game platform", which comprised over 100 sub-systems, based on a detailed, monitored, game economy. Player Experience Design (sample)
Draft for a 4-month player experience renovation plan, in which we rearranged 100 sub-systems in the game for more effective early-stage gameplay learning and better overall experience. ​In the image, one cell stands for one sub-system, while colors represent the types of system, social interaction, resource accumulation, strategic testing, or more. The systems are deployed into different player levels according to the desired player journey. The renovation proved to be successful, with great benefits to early stage retention performance.
Economy Balance Sheet​
The self-developed form was used to control the game's in-game resource balance. Basically, the output (character growth) of each system was carefully designed in accordance with the required input (in-game currency), according to the "value exchange rate" between each resource, which was also carefully calculated. This critically ensured the “fairness” of the entire game, and was the basis of a player's competing and motivation to play.

Up
2012  |  Individual
PC RPG concept work. Adapted story and gameplay from the animation with the same name.​
> Edraw / Photoshop
​> System Architecture / Game System and Level Design / Info Architecture
Up was my first game design, independently conducted to meet the recruiting requirements of XPEC Entertainment.

Throughout the game, I strove for a sense of discovery, trying to extract  the atmosphere from the animation. As the protagonist was an extremely curious but kind boy scout, I abandoned the battles and fighting commonly used in video games, and created a peaceful atmosphere, with a focus on his exploration with his camera picturing wonderful or unusual creatures and scenes along his journey toward "Paradise Falls" (probably his homework for a boy scout's badge..).
​
The work attempts to integrate the animation (Up) and game play, and includes designs of major systems, economic cycles, player tracks, one game level samples, and a rough UI wire frame.
