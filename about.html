About

Chih-Yu Chiang

Aspiration

I love games. I long to exploit this new medium’s true potential to serve society and the economy, and to change ways in which the world sees it. ​As a games designer, I am looking for a shift in the perception of game design, from an artistic view to a more scientific one. Through a systematic study of game values, their connection with designs and mechanisms, and their resonance with other matured academic disciplines, game designs can be improved, and their ability and assist with solving  real world challenges—in management, education, personal growth, policy communication, social issues, and more—will finally be realized.

Experience Summary

Game Experience Research
Game experience research with computational approaches of machine learning, content analysis, natural language processing, and recommender systems.

Gaming
​Industry
Working in the gaming industry turned my long time passion into a reality. Employment by the leading game studio in Great China engaged me in designing a variety of games—role playing, action, card, and SNS—on multiple platforms, polishing my design skills with my specialty in shaping game experience through carefully modeled game economies, by scaffolding character growth, forecasting currency demand and supply, and scheming monetization strategy.
 
My work is made distinctive by my application of business skills to design. In the studio, I initiate the design process paradigm, which allocates my team designated design tasks at each development stage. In the preliminary stage, market analysis, targeting, and player research is necessary, to clearly identify the audience and the experience context that will form the main design strategy. In the creative production stage, sequence planning, design logic, and prototyping are indispensable to set clear-cut target fun types as the guidelines for creativity, and generating the mental effects  that will meet the target players’ needs. In the operational stage, asset management, versioning, and behavior data analysis are vital to create better communication with the team, the publisher, and the future of the product. All these were implemented using Agile methodology to form our daily meetings, sprint goals, reviews, and the team’s solidarity. The  paradigm mitigated design dependency on designers’ personal genius, a source of unstable play experience long damaging the studio’s game quality.
 
Within 18 months the studio had promoted me, only a freshman to this industry, to attend to one of its leading projects as Lead Designer and Deputy Producer. The project collaborated with the studio’s major Chinese partner, Tencent, and became the first profitable web game developed by the company. It achieved a decent market success, with 400 thousand registered players in mainland China, and was remastered to a mobile version and exported to Vietnam and Russia. Because of my proven track record in design and leadership, the studio further charged me with projects with a budget of million-dollars, joining forces with high-profile partners and their lucrative IPs, including Disney’s Marvel and KOEI TECMO’s Dynasty Warriors 8. They exposed me to the internationally avant-garde game design environment, and let me emulate the good, and draw lessons from the bad, from the practices of the most successful developers.
 
Meanwhile, the studio made me Vice Chairman of the Designers’ Committee of Market and Operation. During my tenure, analytical skills were particularly emphasized, so that each designer should be able to work with SQL, VBA, and basic statistics, to manipulate and interpret the data collected from games. Player behavior data mining routines were initiated by my team, with standardized models monitoring payment, retention, and in-game activity logs. For the first time in the studio, scientific information was formally extracted from the operational data and used to form the guidelines for the studio’s future designs.

Business
​Education
My training as part of my Business Master’s program has developed my fundamental understanding of advanced levels of finance, economics, and statistics. I was offered a place in all three top business schools in Taiwan and I was granted the seven national licenses necessary for working in specific financial positions, including security analysis, bond trading, and asset securitization. My business dexterity was developed in strategy, operation, decision science, and, above all, multi-disciplinary knowledge incorporation. I worked on real business solutions with enterprises such as L’Oréal, Diesel, and 3M during my apprenticeships. I also provided over 40 consultations to institutions including industrial leaders in education, telecommunication, and the biotech industry for the Commerce Development Research Institute, a national research think-tank.
 
My Master’s thesis in business explored the issues of knowledge transfer between corporate ventures. Using four decades of worldwide data from prominent business databases, SDC, Compustat, and United States Patent and Trademark Office Database, it highlighted a particular inverted U-shaped relationship between parent institutes’ investment scale and corporate ventures’ innovative performance. Its discovery was honored by the committee, and chosen to be furthered in research by the National Science Council, the country’s highest science agency.
