Analytical Work

Project Operation Dashboard

2015  |  Individual
Dashboard for higher level management and multiple projects concurrent operation. Written in VBA and based on MS Office Excel UI.
> Excel / VBA
​> Project Mgmt / Data Analysis
For the weekly review at XPEC Entertainment, all studio managers had to spend a great amount of time each week collecting, compiling, and organizing project data by hand, since, strangely, the studio did not have software engineer positions for managerial tools’ development.

Seeing the quirky phenomenon, I initiated and built up a multi-project dashboard, which was automated by VBA integration, for managers to undertake concurrent management of published projects' operational performances.
​
It calculated the share and performance of each single project in designated operation periods with metrics and charts, which were then combined together to provide a multi-project summary. With only a few clicks, users were allowed to change the comparison period, switch targets, and produce figures easily with automated processes. The work later became a standard in the studio.

Player Retention Analysis

2013  |  Individual
General framework for player retention data tracking, with operation forms and one sample analytical report.
> Excel / Edraw
​> Project Mgmt / Database Mgmt / Data Mining and Analysis
For retention data monitoring, the Player Retention Model was the first analytical model in the studio to analyze real game data, extract player information for product improvement guidance and, later, period development.

It included data handling, indicator selection and calculation, and dashboard display for quick reference and anomaly alerts. It was first developed in the Tales of the Immortals project, and proposed at the Designer’s Committee of Market and Operation as a studio-wide standard of retention data analysis.

In addition, to introduce the method to the studio, I wrote several analysis reports for the model, providing design suggestions, many of which later proved beneficial to operational performances.
Player Retention Model​
The analytical framework broke down the game journey into several chronological stages (A,B,C..) with corresponding indicators and game systems. This was paired with a dashboard summarizing the metrics for irregular monitoring and fast response.

Game Decomposition - ​Candy Crush Saga

2013  |  Individual
Candy Crush's game mechanism deployment and its success factor analysis from the perspective of ​The Essence of Game Experience.
> Xmind / Edraw
​> System Architecture / Strategic Planning
For a practical verification of the framework (The Essence of Game Experience), I disassembled one of the most popular social games in 2013, Candy Crush Saga, and used the concept organized in The Essence of Game Experience to provide an explanation of its success.

I defined the target audience, broke down the game into mechanism pieces, and analyzed player satisfaction.

Not surprisingly, the work showed a good match for player types (more a Socializer and Achiever) and game mechanisms that provide a high level of mental satisfaction in specific types, which conformed with the framework's conclusion.The work also serves as a template of game analysis in my suggestion.

RPG Turn-based
​Battle Simulator

2012  |  Group of 2
Battle simulator for turn-based RPG design and balancing. Written in VBA based on MS Office Excel UI.​
> Excel / VBA
​> System Design
At the early stage of the Tales of the Immortals project by XPEC Entertainment, there were insufficient coders to support the design tools’ development. I and another designer bought a reference book and built this small app by self-taught VBA.

The app performs RPG turn-based battle simulation, with designated numbers of battle occurrences, character parameters, and skill effects. In Excel, it prints out the results of each character move (ex. attack, use skill), the results of each turn, and the battle's final results. It also calculates both sides' winning probability in a thousand times simulation for generalization.

From the beginning, the app played a crucial role in this game's balancing, especially when the designers trying to evolve new heroes or new skills.
