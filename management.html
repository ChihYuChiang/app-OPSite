Game Development Knowledge Management

2015  |  Individual
Corporate strategy analytical report for XPEC. It includes several suggested initiations concerning game development knowledge management.
> Publisher / Visio
​> Strategic Planning / Proposal / Process Mgmt
Game Development Knowledge Management was a report I provided just before I left XPEC Entertainment. It concerns design issues that were hampering the studio's product quality.

The work presents my observations, together with the problems, solutions and implementation suggestions. My conclusion was that the studio must put more effort into "design knowledge management".

The solution includes three strategic goals: establishing professional design, standardizing the development process, and performing data-oriented plans and feedback, as they represent complete knowledge management of before, during and after, projects.​

Profit and Loss Statement Template

2015  |  Individual
Game development and operation financial forecasting for ROI evaluation.
> Excel
​> Financial Forecasting / Project Mgmt
While most managers in the game industry are professional game developers, with little managerial training, I facilitated their management by leveraging my business background.

One of my several initiations at XPEC Entertainment was a Profit and Loss Statement Template, to formalize a fair and objective method to evaluate game projects and proposals.

​Designed according to this specific studio's needs and game industry characteristics, the statement included information about projected game developmental and operational costs and revenues, with detailed accounts.

Additionally, I separated the key parameters from the statement body, for flexible adjustment. Factors, such as revenue share for IP owners and fees for game platforms, could be adjusted to individual contracts or specific simulation contexts. The statement served as a comprehensive game development financial plan and soon became the studio standard.

Agile Development Rules

2014  |  Individual
Revised Agile Scrum rules which is customized for XPEC's game development teams.
> Edraw
​> Project Mgmt / Process Mgmt
In the past, XPEC's development teams had followed lots of the "Agile rules" with a format, such as daily meetings and weekly reviews, but without any real understanding the methods' real meaning and managerial goals. This damaged the effectiveness of the project method and, sometimes, ironically, invested team resources in unproductive working processes that inversely dragged down the teams' working efficiency.

Seeing this, I conducted a review of the Agile methods and ethos, and redefined the "rules" for my teams. I designed the structure "Scrum of Scrums" that fitted into our working practice, which was based upon "game systems".

A sub-Scrum denoted the development of one game system that operated independently, having its own Agile goals and a complete functional structure, say, responsible designers, artists, and engineers. In the meantime, we had a big Scrum, representing the entire game, containing all sub-Scrums with common design principles and a unified vision.

Product Strategy ​through STP Process

2013  |  Individual
The procedure of applying the STP process in game strategy development, with a real game implementation example.
> Powerpoint
​> Strategic Planning / System Architecture
(Invited lecture in National Open University)

The presentation described the process by which I introduced the business STP concept into game development, with a real example from XPEC Entertainment.

Firstly, I clarified the corporate goals, and attuned the project goals in accordance with them. Secondly, I performed market segmentation and targeting with sufficient research and strategic thinking. Thirdly, I positioned our products with the application of corporate competitive edge. Lastly, I developed a design strategy and game contents according to the guidelines set in the STP procedure.

The STP logic imbued our products with clear vision and brought about a sense of unity that improved game quality, which was elaborated in operation performance.
