Game + Business Project

Employee General Reward System, ​BaoDao Healthcare

Working On  |  Individual
An independent consulting project, integrating game rules and game economy models with a hospital personnel evaluation system and financial bonus calculation.
> Visio / Powerpoint
​> Economy Design / System Design / Proposal / Project Mgmt / Process Mgmt
BaoDao Healthcare is a leading, high-end, private institution for gynecology and obstetrics in Beijing, China, with 500+ personnel.
> Official Site

JiFen (Employee General Reward System) is an employee management tool, which applies game rules to devise a platform, giving rise to competition for boosting positive working attitudes, for more humane services, spontaneous learning and sharing, and a sense of belonging.

Amongst its core is a set of criteria evaluating the range of employee behavior. When creating rivalry, value judgment of each encouraged behavior becomes crucial. It has to be fair enough to be trusted and recognized by all participants. For this reason, the criteria was carefully developed to grade the behavior in three aspects—behavior contribution, intensity, and difficulty—as objectively as possible, quantifying each creditable instance into the system as the competition base and standard.

Moreover, the platform strived for a “complete” reward regime to access more effective behavior and feedback reinforcement by various non-pecuniary remunerations.

Some designs included providing a “JiFen Certificate”, delicately designed tiny paper cards, as instant responses to employees’ good behavior; holding an institution-wide “JiFen Party”, for public commendation in medium reward cycles; and connecting the formal personnel evaluation system with the quantified ethical performance by JiFen, as a basis of long term financial bonuses ($0.75 million per year, about 15% of the salary).
Rules (partial)
These defined the core standard for evaluating and quantifying each encouraged or discouraged behavioural pattern. They define the JiFen application standard procedure and design the principles for connecting with the formal personnel evaluation.

Customer Journey Renovation, ​LuDi

2015  |  Individual
An independent consulting project. It redesigned the restaurant queuing experience with story and virtual world creation, and managed customer complaints with game reward structures.
> Visio / Powerpoint
​> System Design / Info Architecture / Proposal / Project Mgmt / Process Mgmt
Ludi is a Japanese ice cream cuisine in Taichung, Taiwan, famous for “ice monster” mascots.
> Official Site

Via story integration, the project helped to manage gourmands’ sensitivity over extremely long waiting times in popular branches, often around four hours, and utilized the capacity of less busy branches.

The stories, which were derived from the brand, products, and branch physical environments, were carefully released at appropriate times to bring about explorative fun, distracting guests’ feelings of impatience.

Moreover, as customers gradually discovered the stories about “little ice cream monsters” (the product figurines), their lives, their dispositions, and their likes and dislikes, the world the monsters resided in was virtually fabricated, which created meaning to the gastronomic journey. While improving the entire consumption experience, it was integrated into marketing campaigns, applying the concept of collection, setting customers on pilgrimages to the different places (branches) where the monsters lived in for special souvenirs, guiding patrons from popular branches to the less favored ones, as well as maintaining a high level of customer satisfaction.
Project Goals and Approach
This describes the exploratory process of defining the project targets, and developing approaches accordingly, with story, a supportive reward system, and native media interaction.
Renovation Plan (partial)​
Three steps for renovation. Firstly, create a story backbone. Secondly, integrate a created story into each consuming process. Thirdly, leverage stories into the marketing campaign design.

Go! Getting Fit

2013  |  Individual
An independent project. A game-like system helping weight control through game mechanisms that motivate players' “healthy habits”.
> Excel
​> Game Balancing and Economy Design / System Design
Go! Getting Fit helped weight control through game mechanisms designed according to target players’ types.

For the achiever type of players, “spoil points”, a virtual currency accumulated for redeeming on real world splurges, was introduced to reward players accomplishing daily based “healthy habit” goals, such as doing certain rounds of sit-ups or climbing the stairs rather than taking the elevator. It remunerated the players after a small wholesome advancement each day, and helped engender in the players a palpable sense of progression to their long term goal of getting healthier.

On the other hand, for the explorer type of players, the daily goals were especially randomized under specific rules. Unexpected goal types and difficulties led to “surprising” game experiences, forging a sensation of novelty and discovery, creating explorative types of fun.

For grinding players on “healthy habits”, the system attached an extra mental contentment layer upon the habits’ direct costs and benefits, making players less reluctant to avoid doing the exercises.
